# Week 1 Problem Statement: Tata Steel Jamshedpur

## Industry And Internal Context Briefing

Tata Steel Limited's Jamshedpur Works is a flagship integrated steel manufacturing environment where shopfloor capability directly affects safety, quality, uptime, throughput, and customer commitments. L&D is not only a training function in this environment. It is connected to blast furnace stability, steel melting quality, rolling mill consistency, maintenance reliability, safety incidents, onboarding speed, and succession risk.

The common L&D challenge is that training is often planned by calendar, compliance requirement, or manager nomination. That means the organization may know who attended a course, but not whether the right people have the right skills for the right work at the right time.

At the same time, experienced operators and supervisors carry practical know-how that is rarely written down: machine-specific workarounds, quality judgement, maintenance escalation patterns, and "what to watch for" lessons. When those people retire, transfer, or leave, the knowledge loss becomes visible only after mistakes repeat.

## Primary Problem

Leadership and L&D do not have a live, role-level view of critical steel plant skill gaps across roles such as Blast Furnace Operator, Steel Melting Shop Technician, Rolling Mill Operator, and Maintenance Technician. This makes training reactive, generic, and difficult to link to operational performance.

## Current Gap

- Skill matrices are often static, spreadsheet-based, and updated after audits rather than during work.
- Training attendance is tracked more consistently than skill application or competency improvement.
- Supervisors know local skill gaps informally, but that knowledge is not visible across plants, shifts, or functions.
- Content is generic: the same training is assigned to people with different roles, experience, machines, and risk exposure.
- L&D data is disconnected from operational signals such as furnace abnormality, ladle temperature deviation, rolling mill quality variation, breakdown recurrence, safety near misses, and onboarding cycle time.

## Business Impact

- Safety risk: unaddressed skill gaps can increase unsafe work practices and near-miss probability.
- Quality loss: capability variation can affect hot metal quality, heat release decisions, coil quality, rework, and customer-facing defects.
- Productivity loss: teams rely on a few expert operators and technicians to solve repeated operational questions.
- Onboarding drag: new hires take longer to become independently productive.
- Leadership blind spot: investments in training are difficult to prioritize or defend.

## Why Leadership Should Care

If solved, leadership gets a clearer answer to four CXO-level questions:

1. Which critical roles have the highest capability risk?
2. Which training interventions reduce operational risk fastest?
3. Where are we dependent on a few experienced workers?
4. Can we scale skills faster than business complexity is increasing?

## Why AI

AI is relevant because the problem is not only a reporting problem. It requires interpreting messy learning records, role expectations, supervisor observations, assessment results, and knowledge documents, then turning them into personalized actions.

AI can help by:

- summarizing skill evidence from multiple sources
- recommending role-specific learning paths
- answering worker questions in simple language
- converting expert know-how into searchable guidance
- generating supervisor coaching plans
- continuously improving recommendations from feedback and outcomes

## Secondary Opportunity

Capture and reuse tacit knowledge from experienced workers through an AI-assisted knowledge assistant.

This opportunity supports the primary problem because it turns expert know-how into training content, Q&A answers, troubleshooting guides, and scenario-based coaching material.

## Initial Success Metrics

- reduction in time to identify critical skill gaps
- increase in completion of role-relevant training
- reduction in repeat training for already-proficient workers
- faster new-hire time to independent operation
- reduction in repeated process questions to supervisors
- improvement in assessment scores after personalized coaching

## Recommended Problem Statement

Tata Steel Jamshedpur's L&D team needs a live, role-based view of workforce capability and a way to personalize training at the speed of steel plant operations. Without this, training effort is spent broadly instead of precisely, skill risk remains hidden until operational issues appear, and experienced-worker knowledge is lost instead of scaled.
