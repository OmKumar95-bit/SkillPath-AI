# Industry Readiness Checklist

## 1. Problem Fit

- The selected problem must be visible to leadership through safety, quality, downtime, productivity, onboarding, or compliance impact.
- The pilot must focus on a small number of critical roles, not every employee group at once.
- Skill gaps must be tied to role expectations and work outcomes, not only course completion.

## 2. Data Readiness

Required pilot data:

- role-wise skill matrix
- training completion history
- assessment results
- supervisor observations
- approved SOPs and work instructions
- micro-learning content
- quality, downtime, safety, or rework indicators

Data quality checks:

- owner identified for each data source
- refresh frequency defined
- sensitive employee data minimized
- outdated training content flagged
- approved source library separated from draft content

## 3. AI Trust Controls

The assistant should:

- answer only from approved knowledge sources
- show source name or reference
- flag low confidence
- avoid giving unsafe instructions
- escalate safety-critical uncertainty to supervisor or EHS owner
- log unanswered questions for L&D review

The assistant should not:

- replace certification or competency sign-off
- invent procedures
- bypass safety controls
- expose private employee performance data to unauthorized users

## 4. User Experience Controls

For shopfloor workers:

- short language
- role-specific answers
- mobile-friendly layout
- clear escalation steps
- no long theory unless requested

For supervisors:

- coaching priorities
- evidence required after coaching
- team-level patterns
- knowledge capture prompts

For leadership:

- capability risk by role
- link to business outcomes
- pilot ROI estimate
- adoption and trust metrics

## 5. Pilot Success Metrics

Minimum recommended pilot metrics:

- time to identify skill gap
- time to create learning plan
- role-relevant training completion
- supervisor acceptance of recommendations
- worker self-service resolution rate
- number of expert knowledge articles captured
- reduction in repeated questions
- assessment uplift after targeted coaching

## 6. Governance

Required owners:

- L&D product owner
- plant operations sponsor
- EHS or quality reviewer
- data owner
- IT/security owner
- supervisor champions

Review cadence:

- weekly pilot review
- monthly leadership readout
- content safety review before new source release
- post-pilot decision on scale, pause, or redesign
